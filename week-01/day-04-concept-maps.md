## Key formulas

### Limits and derivatives

```math
\lim_{x\to a}f(x)=L
```

```math
f'(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}
```

```math
\frac{d}{dx}x^n=nx^{n-1}
```

Chain rule:

```math
\frac{d}{dx}[u(x)]^n=n[u(x)]^{n-1}u'(x)
```

### Newton’s laws

```math
\sum \vec F=0 \Rightarrow \vec a=0
```

```math
\sum \vec F=m\vec a
```

```math
\vec F_{AB}=-\vec F_{BA}
```

Common force models:

```math
W=mg
```

```math
f_k=\mu_kN
```

```math
F_s=-kx
```

### Periodicity

```math
Z_{eff}=Z-S
```

Across period:

```text
Z_eff increases
atomic radius decreases
ionization energy increases
electronegativity increases
```

Down group:

```text
shell number increases
shielding increases
atomic radius increases
ionization energy decreases
electronegativity decreases
```

## Short ways to understand

- A derivative is the local slope or instantaneous rate.
- Chain rule means “differentiate outside, keep inside, multiply by derivative of inside.”
- A free-body diagram is a force audit.
- Periodic trends come mainly from effective nuclear charge and shell shielding.
