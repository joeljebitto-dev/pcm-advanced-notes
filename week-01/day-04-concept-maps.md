## Math: limits, derivatives, chain rule

Limit:

```math
\lim_{x\to a}f(x)=L
```

Derivative definition:

```math
f'(x)=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}
```

Power rule:

```math
\frac{d}{dx}x^n=nx^{n-1}
```

Chain rule:

```math
\frac{d}{dx}[u(x)]^n=n[u(x)]^{n-1}u'(x)
```

Examples corrected:

```math
\frac{d}{dx}(2x+5)^4=8(2x+5)^3
```

```math
\frac{d}{dx}(x^2+1)^3=6x(x^2+1)^2
```

Short way: chain rule = differentiate outside, keep inside, multiply by derivative of inside.

## Physics: Newton's laws and force models

Newton's laws:

```math
\sum \vec F=0\Rightarrow \vec a=0
```

```math
\sum \vec F=m\vec a
```

```math
\vec F_{AB}=-\vec F_{BA}
```

Force models:

```math
W=mg
```

```math
f_k=\mu_kN
```

```math
F_s=-kx
```

Free-body diagram method:

1. Isolate object.
2. Draw all external forces.
3. Choose axes.
4. Resolve components.
5. Apply `sum Fx=max`, `sum Fy=may`.

Short way: free-body diagrams are force accounting; never mix forces acting on different bodies.

## Chemistry: periodicity and effective nuclear charge

Effective nuclear charge:

```math
Z_{eff}=Z-S
```

Across a period:

```text
Z_eff increases
atomic radius decreases
ionization energy increases
electronegativity increases
```

Down a group:

```text
shell number increases
shielding increases
atomic radius increases
ionization energy decreases
electronegativity decreases
```

Examples corrected:

- Na has larger radius than Cl.
- Mg has larger radius than S.

Short way: across a period the nucleus pulls harder; down a group the valence shell is farther away.
